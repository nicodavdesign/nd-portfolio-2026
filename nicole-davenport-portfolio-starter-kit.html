Project 1
Built Technologies		One-linePayment and compliance platform reducing payment time from 55 days to 27 days
RoleEnd-to-end experience design for developers, general contractors, and subcontractors. Collaborated with PM on product strategy, designed and shipped multiple features validated through user research.
ImpactSubcontractors got paid in 27 days vs. 55-day industry average (51% faster). $290M processed across 6,000+ organizations in 18 months. Payment management became the #1 feature in sales demos. Solved "unsolvable" lower-tier lien waiver collection problem. Created shared platform language across entire construction payment chain.
DescriptionWhen I joined Built, they'd spent a decade focused on lenders. Contractor and subcontractor experiences were designed in silos, creating fragmentation. Through mental modeling, I uncovered the Built Chain insight: developers, contractors, and subs shared nearly identical jobs (collect docs, package payment requests, get paid, pay it forward). I aligned these experiences so Built could move faster by reusing shared functionality, and users could speak the same visual and operational language when collaborating. The breakthrough: we could decouple documents from requirements, giving every stakeholder instant visibility instead of waiting weeks for paper to move up the chain.
Testimonials"Before Built, we spent 1–2 hours tracking down compliance docs and cutting checks. Now we can use that time to focus on project financials and see what's going on." — Melissa Loftin, Project Accountant at Waltz Construction

"Once subcontractors see it work flawlessly the first time and get their money faster, they're happy we switched." — Lisa Augusto, Project Accountant

Design System Evolution		One-lineDesign system evolution positioning design to ship independently
RoleAudited and evolved the existing design system. Introduced tokenized color, typography, and spacing foundations using Token Studio. Established interaction and visual differentiation rules.
ImpactExpanded palette from 32 to 84 WCAG-compliant colors supporting data visualization. Reduced type styles from 11 to 7 with clear hierarchy. 7 production components shipped using updated system. Token Studio integration enables design-to-code sync for modern IDEs. Internal user feedback: "It's certainly more intuitive. Not having seen it before, I naturally fell into the flow of things."
DescriptionWhen I joined, Implentio's design system had been built for MVP based on founder intuition. As I redesigned the core reconciliation workflow, patterns surfaced: accessibility issues, unclear interaction affordances, layouts prioritizing whitespace over scan efficiency in data-heavy views. I restructured the system using Token Studio, separating primitives from semantic intent and creating a stable contract between design and code. This wasn't about perfection. It was about removing friction, supporting clarity, and positioning design to move faster without pulling engineering into every visual change. The system is strong without being rigid, ready to adapt as the business learns and shifts.
Testimonial"It's certainly more intuitive. Not having seen it before, I naturally fell into the flow of things." — Internal customer team member

Project 4
Bambee		One-lineGuided employee termination workflow that turned around Series B NPS
RoleSole designer. Led research with HR managers, designed and shipped the triage flows with state-specific compliance gates.
Impact95% of terminations became self-service, freeing HR managers to focus on the hardest 5%. Turned around Bambee's NPS score. CTO: "The end of employment feature turned around our NPS score."
DescriptionSmall business owners feared legal exposure. One wrong move could put their livelihood at risk. I shadowed Bambee's HR managers and partnered with their compliance expert to bottle their expertise into a self-service system. The insight: some clients tried to soften a firing by calling it a layoff, not realizing the legal risk that created. I designed a three-layer triage (safety → compliance → emotion) that routed high-risk cases to human support while enabling routine terminations to happen confidently without waiting.
Testimonial"The end of employment feature turned around our NPS score." — Mike Ruangutai, former CTO of Bambee

Thermometer icon, empathetic headline ("We're sorry you have to let Bob go!"), and smart defaults tell the whole story. Museum-like negative space composition with centered modal over dimmed background, generous horizontal breathing room.
